Remember Nand2Tetris? A group of brilliant scientists designed this course to help developers like me understand better how computers work from the ground up. They also wrote a book, called Elements of Computing, which I will be exploring among other material in this 6-8 part section of my website. I've always wanted to at least understand how a computer works, if not build one for myself, But having the oppurtunity and the right resource to do the same, and having studied Von Neumann's model in university has always kept me curious about the question:

Im using the nand2tetris as a reference, and I intend to find the time and do the exercises and projects given within the context of the book, alongside my full time job. Which is why this section of my website might seem unfinished and messy at times.

Boolean Logic & Arithmetic

Doing a "minor" in Phsyics these truth tables and boolean logic came in handy while creating digital circuits on bread-boards in order to light up LED's and make adders, multiplexers, and convert analog signals to digital.
